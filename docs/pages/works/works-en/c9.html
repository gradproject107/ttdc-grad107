The Last Piece


If in 2030 we could suppress the shadows of fear in our hearts through technology, through the illusion of constantly mutating memories, would it be a kind of healing or an addiction?

And when the voice of moral criticism of this technology has long been drowned out by a large number of relying on the needs of users, how can the protagonist, who is a law enforcement officer but equally addicted, be forced to rediscover the fear that he does not want to face in an accident?
