Oyster the Purifier | Circular material fabrication


Oyster the purifier is an air purifier that reprocesses oyster shells and develops products through a 3D printing process. It combines the antibacterial and antifungal properties of the material itself to introduce biomaterials into the design of future home appliances, offering a new vision of biomaterials and manufacturing processes.

The main objective of this work is to explore the possibility of applying recycled materials in additive processes. Using the concept of 3D printing of slurry, a large amount of oyster shells accumulated from the coastal area of south-central Taiwan were subjected to high-temperature sintering, sterilization, grinding, and slurry making steps to test the performance of oyster shells after the addition of different natural polymers, with the hope that the opportunities for self-production of recycled materials in digital manufacturing technology can be expanded through experiments in the future.

Oyster slurry, Bio polymer
W250 x H600
